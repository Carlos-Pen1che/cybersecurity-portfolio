# NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

## What is RMF?
The RMF is a step-a-step system used to manage cybersecurity risk in an organization.

---

## RMF Steps

### 1. Prepare
- Explanation: Preparing and organizing resources to handle potential cybersecurity risks before they occur.
- Purpose: To protect the organizational assets and data before a cybersecurity risk occurs.

### 2. Categorize
- Explanation: Classify information and resources according to their impact level if the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA Triad) are compromised.
- Purpose: Define the impact level of information and resources to understand their criticality and make better security decisions, since a misclassification can lead to the implementation of inappropriate controls and compromise the CIA triad.

### 3. Select
- Explanation: Select suitable security controls to mitigate cybersecurity risks.
- Purpose: Select security controls to mitigate risks, since a incorrect selection can lead inadequate or incorrect controls, potentially leaving vulnerabilities or causing unnecessary cost overruns.

### 4. Implement
- Explanation: Implement the security controls selected.
- Purpose: Implement security controls to protect the assets and resources of the organization.

### 5. Assess
- Explanation: Verify that security controls operate effectively
- Purpose: Identify weaknesses in security controls, since incorrect or incomplete verification may fail to detect one or more exploitable vulnerabilities.

### 6. Authorize
- Explanation: An authority approve and accepts risks to allow system operation.
- Purpose: A formal authorization where known risks are accepted to authorize system operation.

### 7. Monitor
- Explanation: Monitor the system 24/7 for potential failures.
- Purpose: It helps anticipate threats and activate incident response, since inadequate monitoring may fail to detect changes, control failures, or emergency threats in timely manner, potentially compromising the CIA Triad

## Practical Example (Applying RMF)

### Scenario
In a hospital, an employee with unnecessary privileges is infected with ransomware, compromising the confidentiality, availability and potential integrity of the patients medical data

### Applying the 7 Steps

- **Prepare:**  Identify and organize the assets and resources that could be compromised during a ransomware attack, identify roles, plan strategies and define an incident response handling the threat.
- **Categorize:**  Classify assets and resources according to impact level to the CIA Triad, SPII of patients as high impact on confidentiality, credentials as high impact on confidentiality and potential integrity, ransomware attack impact on availability of the compromised device.
- **Select:**  Select: Select appropriate security controls to mitigate the problem, for example apply principle of minimum privilege, to reduce the risk from another vulnerabilities based on mismanaged privileges , generate backups outside the network, these controls prioritize the data and resources protection, the confidentiality, integrity and availability of them.
