## NIST CSF (Cybersecurity Framework)

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is a guideline used to manage cybersecurity risk based on five core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover.

## The Five Functions

Identify:
Identify and manage organizational assets, systems, and risks.

Protect:
Develop and implement security measures to prevent potential attacks.

Detect:
Implement SIEM tools and processes to identify cybersecurity events, such as phishing emails or suspicious activity.

Respond:
Take action during a cybersecurity incident to contain and mitigate damage.

Recover:
Restore any affected systems, accounts, and data after an incident.

## Example: Phishing Attack

Identify:
Identify organizational assets and potential targets of phishing attacks.

Protect:
Provide employee training to prevent phishing attacks and reduce risk.

Detect:
Detect phishing attempts through email filtering systems, alerts, or security monitoring tools.

Respond:
If a phishing attack occurs, follow incident response procedures such as isolating affected accounts and reporting the incident.

Recover:
Restore compromised accounts and recover any affected information or systems
