# Scenario: Phishing Attack

## Overview

In an organization, an employee receives an email that appears to be from their boss. The email claims that the employee has earned a bonus and provides a link to claim it. To receive the bonus, the employee is instructed to log in using their business account credentials.

## Assets Affected

The employee’s credentials are at risk, as they may be exposed to the attacker. Additionally, any systems or databases that the employee has access to could be compromised, depending on their level of privileges.


## Threat Type

This is a phishing attack that uses social engineering techniques to trick the employee into revealing their credentials.

## Impact (CIA Triad)

- Confidentiality: High impact, as the employee’s credentials may be exposed, potentially allowing unauthorized access to sensitive company data.

- Integrity: Potential impact, as an attacker with access could modify or manipulate data within the systems the employee has privileges to.

- Availability: Low impact, as this type of attack does not directly affect system availability.

## Analysis

The incident likely occurred due to a lack of security awareness on the employee’s part, particularly regarding phishing and social engineering attacks. This can also reflect a failure on the organization’s side, as insufficient security training increases the likelihood of such incidents.

In this case, the employee became an unintentional internal threat actor after falling victim to a phishing attack that leveraged authority-based social engineering techniques.

This type of issue is closely related to the Security and Risk Management domain, as it involves user awareness, risk mitigation, and organizational security policies.

## Mitigation

This situation could be avoided by applying proper security controls, following frameworks such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, where employee training against threats like phishing is clearly established.

Additionally, this incident shows a failure in following company policies, as the employee compromised confidentiality and potentially put the integrity of company assets at risk.

This highlights the importance of security awareness and compliance within the organization.
